About

Takatso is a community-engaged researcher and emerging scholar whose work centers participatory, justice-oriented approaches to intervention design and program evaluation within the health sector. She is a PhD candidate in Michigan State University’s Ecological/Community Psychology program. Her dedication to the health and social services field dates back to 2009, reflecting extensive experience, knowledge, and a deep understanding of Southern African communities and their ways of life.

Her professional journey spans a wide range of roles. She has contributed to evaluations of global, regional and local initiatives such as the Global Equality Fund (GEF) portfolio, the Hands Off model and its impact on reducing violence and new HIV infections in Southern Africa, and the Going the Last Mile for HIV Control Project and its contribution to ending AIDS as a public health threat by 2030 in Zimbabwe. Her work also includes facilitating life-skills development for teenage girls at Dominican Convent High School and providing psychosocial support to survivors of child sexual abuse at CONTACT Family Counselling Centre, as well as to teenage preterm mothers at the Baby Heroes Africa Foundation and the Lub’elihle Foundation.

Her career is grounded in a commitment to Ubuntu, social justice, and transformative, sustainable change that enhances quality of life. She remains deeply committed to contributing to work that uplifts communities and enhances wellbeing.

Research

My research explores community-engaged, social justice-oriented, and participatory approaches to program evaluation. I’m interested in understanding how community driven evaluation advances transformative, sustainable change in people’s lives through the programs that serve them.

I am also committed to the design and implementation of relevant, culturally responsive, affirming, more inclusive wellness interventions whose evaluation is centered on community priorities, values and context.

My work focuses on wellness interventions such as life skills development, mental health and sexual and reproductive health and rights(SRHR), gender-based violence (GBV) programs.
