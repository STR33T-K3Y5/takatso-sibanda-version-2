About Me.

Community-engaged researcher and community psychologist with extensive experience across health and social service sectors. Specializes in community-centered, rights-based approaches to wellness promotion that elevate local insight and lived experience in the design, implementation, and evaluation of well-being initiatives. Experienced in partnering with marginalized communities, civil society organizations, government ministries, healthcare providers, and interdisciplinary teams. Research interests include narrative inquiry, arts-based and ethnographic methods, and storytelling as tools for community engagement and evaluation.

Creator of the ethnographic documentary *Becoming Who I Am* (2023), which explores intersections of gender identity, neurodiversity, culture, disability, sexuality, and embodiment. Currently a Graduate Research Assistant on the Promoting Rights-Based Evaluation project, collaborating internationally to transform evaluation practice by centering local expertise, priorities, and values.
